# Trust And Safety

## Main threats

### Fake accounts

Political groups, paid farms, or hostile users may create many accounts to manipulate ratings.

Mitigation:

- Identity verification levels
- Device and behavior risk checks
- Trust score based on history
- Rate limits
- Manual review for viral reports

### Fake reviews

Public rating can become mob voting.

Mitigation:

- Keep public feedback below 20% of leader score
- Weight feedback by verified local residency and account trust
- Detect duplicate language and coordinated timing

### False criminal record interpretation

Cases can be political, false, pending, or already dismissed.

Mitigation:

- Separate `convicted`, `charges framed`, `pending`, `acquitted`
- Always show source links
- Do not reduce score only by raw case count

### Defamation and abuse

Users may post personal attacks.

Mitigation:

- Strict content policy
- Evidence-first reporting
- No personal insults
- Moderation queue
- Right to reply and correction workflow

### Platform bias

If the platform recommends candidates, it may become propaganda.

Mitigation:

- Show facts and score methodology
- Avoid voting recommendations
- Publish rating formula
- Keep audit logs
- Allow independent review of data changes

## Reporter trust score

A reporter trust score can increase when:

- Reports are verified
- Evidence is original
- Locations are accurate
- Past reports were solved or confirmed

It should decrease when:

- Reports are fake
- Evidence is misleading
- Duplicate spam is repeated
- Political slogans replace factual reporting
